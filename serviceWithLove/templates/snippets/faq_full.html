9. Is the organizing work done by the Professional Organizer or the client?
The purpose of the Professional Organizer is to provide the necessary skills to the client, helping them to establish organization strategies and build proficiency in their organizing skills. That is why the ideal session has the Professional Organizer work together with the client. There may be cases in which either the Professional Organizer or the client works alone, but this will be established at the evaluation interview and/or throughout the organization project.
10. Apart from hiring the services of ORGANIZING WITH LOVE, should I invest in products for organizing?
The conditions in which the organization task is carried out must be adapted not only to the abilities of each client but also to their budget. These solutions will be established at the evaluation interview and/or throughout the organization project.

14. How do you know when it’s time to get organized?
  • You spend at least a few minutes every morning in mild panic because you can’t find something you need.
  • You need to rearrange the things in your kitchen so that you can get to the things you need. For example, you must move a device out of the way to use another device.
  • You need to shuffle a lot of outfits in your closet before coming up with something that really fits you.
  • You have thrown your shoes on the floor of your closet because you cannot deal with the pile.
  • You fold and fold, but your drawers always end up in a big mess.
  • You realize that most of your toiletries and cosmetics are rarely used
  • You just keep adding new mail to the existing stack every day. It is too overwhelming to face.
  • Piles of miscellaneous papers/children’s artwork/newspapers are the first thing you see when you walk into a room.
  • You can never find a pen/the scissors/the good knife when you want them.
  • Things are not kept in logical places and never go back to where they belong.
  • You cannot use a storage area for its intended purpose. Your car won’t fit in the garage, your desk drawer is so full of old bills that it can’t fit anything else inside.
  • The way you store things has caused something to be broken or damaged.
  • Flat surfaces such as countertops and tables are stacked with papers, books, clothing, and other sundries.
  • You have no idea what’s behind the cabinet.
  • You have more furniture dedicated to storage than dedicated to places to sit and relax.
  • Opening a closet, drawer or cabinet fills you with anxiety because things are likely to fall out.
  • You feel cramped in your home due to the amount of things that take up space.
  • When you get home at the end of the day, there is no place for your things to rest at night.
  • You have considered moving to have more space for your things.
  • You are discouraged by the appearance of your home.
